Data Research#2: Running My First Program

Hello world,

Welcome back to my blog once again. After arriving with the research question whether ethnicity plays a role in ones’ outlook, both on their own life and country, I have further taken a closer look on Outlook on Life Survey (OOL) survey data from 2,294 respondents.

From all data available, I have picked out 3 variables that are crucial to the question, namely:

1. PPETHM which is the race/ethnicity of the respondents categorizing to 5 groups which are
     1 - White, Non-Hispanic
     2 - Black, Non-Hispanic
     3 - Other, Non-Hispanic
     4 - Hispanic
     5 - 2+ Races, Non-Hispanic

2. W1_P2 which is the social class respondents think they belong to by answering to the question “People talk about social classes such as the poor, the working class, the middle class, the upper-middle class, and the upper class. Which of these classes would you say you belong to?”
     1 Poor
    2 Working class
    3 Middle class
    4 Upper-middle class
    5 Upper class
    -1 Refused to answer (classified as missing data)

3. W1_F1 which is the respondents’ outlook of their own like by replying to question “When you think about your future, are you generally optimistic, pessimistic, or neither optimistic nor pessimistic?”
     1 Optimistic
    2 Neither
    3 Pessimistic
    -1 Refused to answer (classified as missing data)

After all these data exploration, the summary on these variables are:

1. Most of the respondents (90%) are Black and White ethnicity with roughly 55% Black, 35% White and 10% shared by Hispanic, other ethnicity and people with more than 2 ethnicity, respectively. There’s no missing value in this part.

2. Majority of the respondents (75%) think they belong to middle or working class which account for 42% and 33% of respondents. Interestingly, the size of the respondent perceiving themselves as ‘poor’ has similar number as those who think of themselves as ‘upper-middle class and above’. The respondents who refused to answer this question account for 4%.

3. More than half (55%) of the respondents still have positive outlook on their future with roughly 35% of them having neutral outlook (neither optimistic nor pessimistic) and 10% being negative. This could be interpreted on both ways. On one hand, one might say that country with only half of citizen having positive life outlook does not seems to be doing well. On the other hand, one could also claim that more than half being optimistic is ‘fair enough‘ given the economic situation in 2012. The respondents who refused to answer this question account for 2%.

As usual, I will be in touch when more analysis is reached.

*This post is part of the assignment for Data Management and Visualization course as part of Learn Data Science Fundamentals Specialization by Wesleyan University in Coursera using Outlook on Life Survey (OOL) dataset which was designed to study political and social attitudes in the United States mainly to consider the ways in which social class, ethnicity, marital status, feminism, religiosity, political orientation, and cultural beliefs or stereotypes influence opinion and behavior.
