Data Research#1: Getting Your Research Project Started

Hello world, 

This is my first time blogging and I’m more than happy to welcome all of you here.

This post is part of the assignment for Data Management and Visualization course as part of Learn Data Science Fundamentals Specialization by Wesleyan University in Coursera. 

Based on the assignment, everyone is supposed to formulate the research question from one of five datasets available which are
1. The U.S. National Epidemiological Survey on Alcohol and Related Conditions (NESARC)
2. The Mars Craters Study
3. The National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent Health (AddHealth)
4. The portion of the GapMinder data which includes one year of numerous country-level indicators of health, wealth and development.
5. The Outlook on Life Survey (OOL)

After looking through all codebooks, I have decided to have a go with the Outlook on Life Survey (OOL) dataset which was designed to study political and social attitudes in the United States mainly to consider the ways in which
social class, ethnicity, marital status, feminism, religiosity, political orientation, and cultural beliefs or stereotypes influence opinion and behavior.

The question that I came up is simple, but not easy, does ethnicity plays a role in ones’ outlook, both on their own life and country? If so, how? My starting hypothesis would be that ethnicity played a role in outlook on life and country while main variables I will be looking at are respondents’ ethnicity, their outlook on life and US future (optimistic, pessimistic, neither) and country’s economy (better, worse, same).

After the second review, I think that the other variables that might influence the outlook would be social status since the ethnicity bu itself might not makes someone optimistic/pessimistic, but rather what they thought of themselves.

My search terms are ethnicity, outlook, social, socioecomic, status, america.

After performing literature review via Google Scholar, there exists some interesting findings:

1. On one hand, few researchers agreed that ethnicity and social status does contribute to one’s mental health and life outlook (Chong and Kim 2006 [1], Burke-Miller 2010 [2]), although they have different ideas on the the mechanism and other interesting details.
Chong and Kim claim that ethnicity effect is largely caused by individual experiences from discrimination perceptions, not directly by the ethnicity group itself. They also shown that, among ethnic groups, African American are less responsive to changes in economic circumstances because they tend to be more pessimistic about their life prospects and more likely to encounter discrimination. They further stated that among those minority individuals who experience equal opportunity and less discrimination, higher economic status often leads to a reduced emphasis on race and ethnicity.

Burke-Miller view is that the social status effect is especially significant in women, whose mental health is influenced by their vulnerable social status, unique social roles, and over-representation among the urban poor.

2. On the other hand, there’s also researcher who is against the idea (Williams 1996) suggesting that social status fail to completely account for racial variations in health status and more attention is needed for the unique factors linked to race that affect health. These factors include racism, migration, acculturation, and the comprehensive assessment of social status. 

This seems like a good intro before digging deeper in the topic. I will be in touch when more analysis is reached.

[1]:  Chong, D., & Kim, D. (2006). The Experiences and Effects of Economic Status Among Racial and Ethnic Minorities. Am. Pol. Sci. Rev. American Political Science Review, 100(03).
[2]:  Burke-Miller, J. K. (2010). Social and Community Contexts. A Public Health Perspective of Women’s Mental Health, 335-345. 
[3]:  Williams, D. R. (1996). Race/Ethnicity and Socioeconomic Status: Measurement and Methodological Issues. International Journal of Health Services, 26(3), 483-505.
